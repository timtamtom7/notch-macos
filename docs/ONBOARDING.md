# Notch — Onboarding

4 screens for the notch/menu bar widget utility.

---

## Screen 1 — Welcome to Notch

**Headline:** Your notch, useful.

**Body:** Notch fills your MacBook's display cutout with glanceable widgets — time, battery, weather, and more — always visible, never in the way.

**Illustration:** A stylized MacBook screen viewed head-on, with the notch clearly visible at the top center. Inside the notch, a small pill-shaped bar shows mini widgets (a time "9:41", a battery icon, a sun icon). The rest of the screen is clean and dark, emphasizing the notch area. Minimal, clean, product-forward.

**CTA Button:** "Get Started →"

**Skip link:** "Skip setup" (bottom right)

---

## Screen 2 — Choose Your Widgets

**Headline:** Pick what you want to see.

**Body:** Toggle on the widgets that matter to you. Drag to reorder. Notch will sit quietly in your display area until you need it.

**Illustration:** A mockup of the Notch widget bar, expanded into a preferences panel. Inside: a list of widget toggles (Time, Date, Battery, Weather, Keyboard Layout) with toggle switches. Each widget has a small preview icon on the left. Clean card with rounded corners, soft shadow.

**CTA Button:** "Continue →"

---

## Screen 3 — Position & Appearance

**Headline:** Fine-tune how it looks.

**Body:** Notch auto-detects your MacBook model. If your display has a notch, it sits right there. On other Macs, it appears as a slim bar at the top of your screen. Adjust opacity and which display to use.

**Illustration:** A comparison illustration showing two scenarios side by side:
- Left: A MacBook with a visible notch, the Notch widget bar sitting inside it
- Right: A desktop Mac display with a thin top bar

Both showing the widget bar in the appropriate position. Below: an opacity slider mockup (currently at 80%).

**CTA Button:** "Next →"

---

## Screen 4 — All Set

**Headline:** Notch is live.

**Body:** Notch is running in your menu bar. Click it anytime to change settings. Press `⌘⇧N` to toggle visibility.

**Illustration:** A close-up illustration of just the notch area on a MacBook screen, now showing the Notch bar active and live with real-looking data (a time like "3:45 PM", a battery icon at 87%, a sun icon with "72°"). A small checkmark badge in teal. Clean, functional, done.

**CTA Button:** "Open Notch Settings"
